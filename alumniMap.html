Alumni

Happy New Year, Gaines Alumni!

As today is the start of a new semester at UK, it seems like the perfect time to check in and say hello.

Thanks to so many of you for filling out our questionnaire and sending us your news! Lisa is hard at work compiling all of your biographical information into a user-friendly format that will make it easy for you to see where alums find themselves, and what they’re up to. We’d love to hear from all of you, so if you have not already sent us a brief update, simply click here to do so.

I’m delighted to share with you a documentary about the Gaines Center that Kendra Sanders (Gaines class of 2015) produced for her jury project.  You can watch it here. I think it will remind you of what makes the Gaines experience so special!

I’m also happy to advise you to save the date: Gaines reunion picnic: Saturday, April 30, 5-10pm at the Gaines Center. Please come and catch up with each other and help current fellows as well as current and past directors honor Ron Pen. Tomorrow, Ron, having entered phased retirement, will begin teaching his final spring Gaines seminar unit. This marks the end of a long and truly wonderful era at Gaines, so we plan to sing Ron’s praises at the reunion picnic. He promises to bring his fiddle!  We’ll send out a more detailed announcement and an RSVP as we get a little closer to the date.

Very many thanks indeed to those of you who’ve contributed to the Gray Art Appreciation fund. With the aid of the Gaines Board’s development subcommittee, we’ve launched a fundraising campaign to replenish the Gray Fund, so that we can keep alive the now 15-year tradition of sending the senior fellows for a cultural weekend in New York City. For full details of our needs, and for how to give, just click this link.

May 2016 bring peace, health, and happiness to you and yours!

Cheers, Phil

 

 

 

Phil Harling
Gaines Professor of the Humanities
Distinguished Service Professor of History
Director, Gaines Center for the Humanities
